  1862 Birth of American soprano Geraldine ULMAR in Boston. d-13 AUG 1932. Was amateur
singer in concerts as a child. Her professional
stage début was on a ship in a lake in Boston's Oakland Garden as
[Josephine] in H.M.S. Pinafore, August (1879). Boston Ideal Opera Company as leading soprano for six years. With Sir Arthur Sullivan and as
 [Yum-Yum] with Carte's First American Mikado
Company, opening at the Fifth Avenue Theatre, NYC, in August
(1885). Continuing same performance in Liverpool, Manchester, and
then appeared in Germany before returning to the U.S. She appeared
in productions of Princess Ida, The Mikado, and Patience. She
returned to England and the D'Oyly Carte to rehearse opera,
Ruddygore. She remained there until (1904), after appearing as [Jane
Jingle] Ladyland she retired from the stage and devoted herself to
teaching singing. m-briefly, in the 1890s, to
composer-conductor Ivan Caryll.
