  1870 Birth of Belgian bass-baritone Hector Dufranne, in Mons. d. Paris, May 4, 1951: He studied at the Brussels Conservatory making his début at the Monnale, Brussels (1898), from a modest tannery worker, into the first rank of this century's performers. A native of Mons, until 1939, he made the round of the best known stages in the world. His singing belonged to the old school in which the language was not allowed to compromise vocal quality, two years later he arrived at the O-CO, where he remained for nearly forty years. He sang at Monte Carlo, CG, 1914, New York, Chicago where he was a regular member until 1922, missing only one season. His career continued until the outbreak of the Second World War, and his final performance was at Vichy in 1939.
