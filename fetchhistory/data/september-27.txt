 <li>
  1871 Birth of German tenor Alois BURGSTALLER in Holzkirchen. d-19 APR 1945. Watchmaker and singer he was encouraged by Cosima Wagner to train at the Bayreuth School. He made his stage début there in 1894. Roles included Siegfried in Siegfried (1896).  He also sang in Paris, Brussels and various German cities. Was the first Parsifal at the Metropolitan Opera (1903), which drew the wrath of Cosima Wagner, who wished to gain exclusive rights for the work.  As a result, Burgstaller, as well as basso Anton van Rooy, and conductor Alfred Hertz, were barred from any further performances at Bayreuth. By 1908, his voice deteriorated and he returned to Germany
