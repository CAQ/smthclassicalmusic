1849. The founder of the Vienna Philharmonic first achieved fame in Italy. He was appointed musical director at Vienna's "Kärntnerthor Theater" in 1837-38 season and was named Court Kapellmeister in 1841. Operas, "Enrico"
(original title, "Rosmonda d'Inghilterra" Trieste, 1839); "Il
Templario" (Turin, 1840, [rev] as "Der Tempelritter" Vienna, 1845,
[rev] as "Der Tempelritter" Vienna, 1845); "Gildippe ed Odoardo"
(Genoa, 1840, losr); "Il proscritto" (Milan, 1841, [rev] as "Die
Heimkehr des Verbannten" Vienna, 1846, [rev] as "Il
proscritto); "Die lustigen Weiber von windsor" (Berlin, 1849).
