  1902 Birth of Russian tenor  Lemeshev, Sergei [Yakovlevich] (t) (1902-1977) Russ. (76) b. Staroe
Knydzevo, Russia, July, 10, 1902; d. Moscow, June 26, 1977:
Lemeshev became one the leading tenors at the Bolshoi Opera between
(1930-50). He hd a bright, lyric tenor voice. He sang a wide
variety of operas, including the standard Russian repertoire, as
well as French, Italian and German lyric roles, all sung in
Russian. He also enjoyed great success not only on the Russian
opera and concert stage, but in cinema as well. Début:: Sverdlovsk
(1926) Kharbin Opera in Manchuria (1927-29); Tiflis Opera (1929-31);
Bolshoi (1931). He retired in 1961, and taught in the Moscow
Conservatory. At the Bolshoi, on his 70th birthday, he sang [Lensky]
Eugene Onegin (1972). Debut  1926
